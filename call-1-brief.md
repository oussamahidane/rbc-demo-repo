# Call 1 Brief — Mentee Intel Call

**Goal:** intel + referral mechanics. **NOT the intro ask** — that's Call 2, after you've submitted. Asking for anything today makes the call about you; keeping it curiosity-only makes vouching for you later feel like his idea.

**Tone:** former mentor catching up, not a candidate interrogating. He owes you goodwill — let him spend it naturally.

## Flow

**1. Open (5 min):** Genuine catch-up. How's the team, how's the role treating him, what he's working on. Don't rush this — the intel quality depends on him relaxing into it.

**2. The pivot (one line):**
> "I saw the AI Adoption Analyst posting on your team — I'm seriously looking at it. It's basically the exact intersection of what I've been building and where I'm deliberately headed. Before I apply, can I pick your brain?"

Three jobs in one line: "before I apply" signals you're applying regardless (no pressure on him), it legitimizes every question after it, and it seeds the locked frame — see Rules below for why that matters even though he knows your story.

**3. The five questions, in conversational order:**

| # | Ask it like this | Listen for |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | "What's behind the posting — did something break or stall?" | The real pain. Named tools that flopped, adoption that died, a PM revolt. This becomes the deck's hypothesis slide. |
| 2 | "Is it a genuine external search, or is someone internal already pencilled in?" | Hesitation = earmarked. Changes effort allocation, not the decision to apply. |
| 3 | "What's the team actually using day to day? What stuck, what got abandoned?" | Tool names, failed rollouts, who the skeptical PMs are and why. |
| 4 | "What does your boss actually care about? How does he size people up?" | Decision style, pet peeves, what impresses him. Calibrates Meeting 1 entirely. |
| 5 | "If down the line you referred me — does RBC need that logged before I submit, or can it attach after?" | **The one answer you cannot leave without.** This sets your submission date. |

Q5 phrasing matters: "if down the line" floats the referral as hypothetical — he can offer it, you're not asking for it.

**4. Close (no ask):**
> "This is really helpful. Let me get my application in properly first — I might come back to you once it's in."

Plants Call 2 without requesting anything today.

## Rules

- Don't pitch — he already knows your work intimately. The call is pure intel + mechanics.
- **Seed the frame anyway.** He knows the facts, but his *retelling* is what reaches the boss, and the version in his head today may be "startup wound down, he's looking for work." You're not informing him — you're overwriting the words he'll use. The pivot line does this; don't belabor it beyond that.
- Never any version of the desperation frame, even with him — especially with him. He repeats what he hears.
- If he offers the intro unprompted: "I'd love that — give me a few days to get you something forwardable." Don't accept a same-day forward with nothing in his hands.

## Fallbacks

- Call not booked by **Jul 15** → text him Q5 alone.
- No answer by **Jul 18** → submit anyway. Warm path supplements, never blocks.
